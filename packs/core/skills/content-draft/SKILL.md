# content-draft - draft content from what the company knows

Turns real material in the brain (a shipped result, a decision, a customer story) into a draft for
LinkedIn, a blog, or an update - so content is a byproduct of operating, not a separate chore.

## When to use

- There's a real result, lesson, or milestone worth sharing.
- The team wants a first draft to react to, not a blank page.

## Steps

1. Find the source material in the brain (a decision, a review, a client outcome) and cite it.
2. Draft in the company's voice (see `conventions.md` for tone); lead with the specific, true thing.
3. Save to `content/drafts/<slug>.md` with the source links kept.
4. Note where it would publish - but do not publish.

## Rules

- Grounded and specific over clever; every claim traces to something real.
- Publishing is an outward action - always a human tap, never automatic.
- Respect confidentiality: no client detail without their material already being shareable.
