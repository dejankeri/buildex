# The exponential-org method

The method BuildEx installs. It is small on purpose: a company is a set of self-improving loops around
one brain, and the job is to make those loops safe, fast, and mostly automatic.

## The loop

Every recurring piece of company work is one loop:

**Sensor → Policy → Tools → Quality gate → Learning**

- **Sensor** - something enters: an email, a form, a metric, a message. Connectors bring the exhaust
  into `sources/`.
- **Policy** - what the company decided to do about this kind of thing (its conventions, its verbs).
- **Tools** - the agent does the work directly on the brain's files.
- **Quality gate** - anything outward or irreversible waits for a human tap. Reads and drafts flow.
- **Learning** - the outcome and any new decision are captured back into the brain.

The brain is the one shared substrate all the loops turn around. It only gets better.

## What humans do

Humans sit at the edge of the loops. They **approve** (the gate), they are the **face** (outward
relationships), and they make the **novel, high-stakes calls**. The scarce resource in an
AI-native company is approval-hours, not build-hours - so the whole system is designed to spend the
fewest human taps for the most leverage.

## How an engagement installs this

1. **Map** the company's loops, data sources, and automatable surface.
2. **Stand up** the workspace + connect the agent + wire connectors.
3. **Backfill** the brain from existing knowledge; `tidy` files it to conventions.
4. **Install** the kernel (rules + policy) and the first verbs.
5. **Operate** - the team runs their week in the app; new verbs and connectors accrete over time.

Nothing here is speculative machinery. The stack is git + markdown + the operator's own agent + a
gate. The craft is in the seamlessness, not the novelty.
