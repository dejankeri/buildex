The theory

The exponential organization.

For the first time, a company's output can stop tracking its headcount. The work compounds in a shared brain instead of walking out the door each evening.

Here's the idea - and how you actually run one.

Models are a commodity. The brain is the asset.

The mechanism

A company is a set of self-improving loops.

Sensor → Rules & Skills → Tools → Gate → Learning, cycling around one brain. The agent runs the middle; you stand at the edge.

Every turn writes back what it learned - so the loop gets better each time it runs. That's the compounding.

one loop, compounding
Safety is the unlock

Wide open by default. The sharp edges wait for you.

Once you ask, the agent works unsupervised across your files, the web, and your connected tools. Only the sharp edges - moving money, messaging real people, publishing, anything you can't undo - wait for your ok. Every outward move is logged.

And it's a dial you turn: widen a gate you trust, tighten one you don't.

The new scarce unit

Approval-hours, not build-hours.

When agents run the middle of every loop, the bottleneck isn't headcount - it's how many good decisions a human can make. Spend approval-hours where judgment matters; spend none where it doesn't.
